Half the homes in Highlands Ranch, Parker, and Castle Rock were built on terrain that rolls — which means walk-out basements, and decks that stand a full story off the ground. If that's your house, every generic deck-cost article you've read is quietly wrong for you. Height changes the math, and here's how.

## The short answer

For a capped-composite elevated deck in the Denver metro in 2026, plan on **15–30% above ground-level pricing** — call it **$46–$90 per square foot installed**, against $40–$70 for the same deck at grade. Typical projects:

| Project | Typical installed cost |
|---|---|
| 300 sq ft walk-out deck | $14,000–$27,000 |
| 400 sq ft second-story deck with stairs | $20,000–$40,000 |
| Two-tier build (upper deck + finished lower patio) | $35,000–$65,000+ |

## Where the extra money goes

None of it is markup for altitude drama — it's structure you can point at:

- **Posts and beams.** A deck ten feet up carries the same loads through much longer posts, which means larger members, more bracing, and connections engineered for the leverage.
- **Footings.** Taller structure concentrates bigger loads on each footing, and Colorado frost depth means every one is dug and poured properly regardless — there's just more riding on each.
- **Railings that are truly fall protection.** At second-story height, the railing isn't trim — it's the thing between a guest and a ten-foot drop. Code treats it that way and so do we.
- **Stairs to grade.** A staircase from a second-story deck is a small structure in its own right — stringers, landings, its own footings and railing. Budget **$2,000–$4,500+** depending on the run; our [stairs and railings guide](/blog/deck-stairs-railings-cost-guide) goes deeper.
- **Labor and access.** Everything happens off ladders and scaffolds, slower and more carefully than deck work at grade.

## The walk-out bonus: the space underneath

Here's the part that flips the math back in your favor: an elevated deck creates a shaded, rain-protected room under it for a fraction of what that square footage costs anywhere else on your property. A gravel or paver pad, a ceiling system or simple under-deck drainage, a fan and lights — and the deck you bought for upstairs dining doubles your usable outdoor space. Our [two-tier builds](/portfolio/two-tier-balcony-deck) and [covered walk-out projects](/portfolio/walkout-covered-deck) are the pattern: dining up top, lounge in the shade below. It's the single best value-add in elevated deck design, and it's why we rarely build a walk-out deck without at least roughing in the space beneath.
