## Your neighbor, not just your contractor

Haka Decks is headquartered in Centennial, in the heart of the greater Denver Tech Center — our shop sits off Easter Lane near Peoria, a few minutes from Centennial Airport. When we build in Willow Creek, Piney Creek, Foxridge, or Smoky Hill, we're working in our own backyard. That matters for more than sentiment: it means we know what Centennial's homes need, we can be on site quickly during a build, and we're still nearby years later if you ever need us.

## Centennial custom decks, designed where they're built

"Custom" gets thrown around; here it's literal. Every one of our Centennial custom decks starts with a deck designer standing in the actual yard — checking the sun path, the grade, the sight lines to the neighbors — before anything gets drawn. Then the same company runs the deck construction: our own crew, our own project lead, from the first footing inspection to the final walkthrough.

Most deck installation in Centennial is [capped composite](/services/composite-decks), because that's what this climate rewards. But we're not a one-material shop — [wood deck installation](/services/hardwood-cedar-decks) in cedar or hardwood is still on the menu for homeowners who want the real thing and accept the maintenance that comes with it. Either way the framing underneath is pressure-treated, code-current, and engineered for our snow and wind loads; the surface is a choice, the structure isn't.

## What Centennial homes ask of a deck

Most of Centennial's housing stock went up between the 1970s and the 1990s, which means a large share of the decks on those homes are original or second-generation wood builds now reaching the end of their lives. We replace a lot of them. The pattern is consistent: south- and west-facing decks bleached and split by high-altitude sun, fasteners worked loose by freeze-thaw, and framing that predates today's code. Our usual answer is a [capped composite rebuild](/services/composite-decks) on new or reinforced framing — a deck that shrugs off the sun and hail that killed the last one.

Mature trees are the other Centennial signature. Lots here have forty years of growth on them, and a good deck design works around a canopy rather than clearing it. Multi-level layouts, wrapped corners, and integrated [pergolas and patio covers](/services/pergolas-patio-covers) all show up often in our Centennial work — along with slim [cable and aluminum railing](/services/railings) that keeps the sightlines open through the trees.

Not every aging deck needs to come out, either. When the framing underneath is genuinely sound we'll say so and quote a [repair or re-deck](/services/deck-repair) rather than a full [replacement](/services/deck-replacement) — it's the same inspection either way, and you get the answer in writing.

## How deck construction in Centennial actually runs

The process is the same whether it's a 200-square-foot re-deck or a two-tier build with a kitchen:

1. **Initial consultation** — free, at your house, usually within the week. We measure, talk budget honestly, and look at what your yard wants.
2. **Design and quote** — drawings from our deck designer, material samples you can hold, and a fixed number — not an estimate that grows.
3. **Permits and HOA** — we produce the plans, file with the city, and handle your HOA's architectural review while you do nothing.
4. **Construction** — most Centennial decks are installed in one to three weeks on site, with the same crew start to finish.
5. **Walkthrough and warranty** — we walk every line of the build with you, then stand behind it in writing.

Because the shop is a few minutes away, mid-build questions get answered on site the same day — not by phone from across the metro.

## Permits and HOAs in Centennial

Deck work in Centennial runs through the city's building division, and most established neighborhoods — Willow Creek, Piney Creek, Foxridge, and their neighbors — have active HOAs with architectural review on top of that. We handle both: drawings, permit submission, HOA paperwork, and inspections are part of every Haka build, not an extra. If you want the background, our [permit guide for Centennial and the surrounding counties](/blog/deck-permits-centennial-arapahoe-douglas) walks through the whole process.
