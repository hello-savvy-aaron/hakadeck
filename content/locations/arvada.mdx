## Two Arvadas, two different jobs

Arvada is old and new at the same time, and which one you live in decides what your deck project actually is.

East of Wadsworth, around Olde Town, Lake Arbor, and the blocks that filled in through the 1950s, '60s, and '70s, the housing is mature and so are the decks. Many are original or second-generation wood, and at Colorado altitude that means bleached, split boards, fasteners worked loose by freeze-thaw, and framing that predates current code. That's replacement country.

West of Indiana, the story flips. Candelas, Leyden Rock, Five Parks, West Woods and the communities that grew out toward the foothills over the last two decades sit on young lots with almost no canopy — and their decks are usually fine structurally but far too small and far too sunny for how the houses live. That's shade-and-expansion country.

## The east-side work: honest replacement

On the mature side we do a lot of teardown-and-rebuild: strip the tired structure, rebuild on code-current framing, and finish in [capped composite](/services/composite-decks) that outlasts the roof. The part that matters most is the part nobody sees — the ledger connection where the deck meets the house is where old decks fail, and it's the first thing we look at.

We inspect before we quote. Sound framing gets a [re-deck](/services/deck-repair) instead of a full [replacement](/services/deck-replacement), and that saves real money. When the structure genuinely needs to come out, we say that too, in writing.

## The west-side work: shade that can take the wind

Out toward the foothills, the request is almost always the same — this deck is unusable after three o'clock. A [pergola or patio cover](/services/pergolas-patio-covers) is the fix, and on that side of Arvada it can't be a flatland detail. West Arvada catches genuine downslope wind off the foothills, which loads a roofed structure upward rather than down. Engineered post connections, correctly sized footings, and hardware specified for the exposure are what separate a cover that lasts from one that becomes a neighbor's problem in a spring windstorm. It's the same engineering our [Golden](/locations/golden) builds get, a few miles east.

The other common west-side project is simply more deck. Production builders ship a minimum platform; five or ten years in, people want the outdoor room the house always deserved — extended footprints, a lower level under a walk-out, built-in seating, and [railing](/services/railings) slim enough to keep the mountain views the west side is bought for.

## Permits and HOA review

Inside city limits, Arvada's building division is your counter — most of the city sits in Jefferson County, with the northeast corner reaching into Adams, but the city handles the permit either way. The newer communities add HOA architectural review ahead of that, with real standards for materials, colors, and how a structure reads from neighboring lots.

We prepare both packets, submit them, and run the inspections. It's part of every build, not an add-on, and our [permit guide](/deck-permits-south-denver-metro) covers how the process generally works if you want the background.
