## Two Lakewoods, two kinds of deck job

Lakewood is large enough to be several towns at once, and for our purposes it's two. East of Wadsworth, the mid-century neighborhoods around Belmar and Kendrick Lake are full of 1950s-to-1970s ranch homes on flat, generously treed lots — and a striking number still carry an original or second-generation wood deck. That's replacement country. Applewood, up in the northwest corner, is the same story with bigger lots and older trees.

West of that, the ground tilts. Green Mountain, Solterra, and the blocks running toward Bear Creek and the hogback sit on real grade, with walk-out basements and views back across the metro or straight into the foothills. That's elevated-deck country, and it's a different engineering problem entirely.

## The replacement work

On the flat, mature side of town, most of what we do is honest replacement: tear out a tired wood structure, rebuild on code-current framing, and finish in [capped composite](/services/composite-decks) that outlasts the roof. Colorado is hard on a forty-year-old redwood deck — altitude UV bleaches and dries it, freeze-thaw backs the fasteners out, and hail seasons finish the job — and by the time a deck feels spongy underfoot the ledger usually deserves a look too.

We inspect before we quote. If the framing is genuinely sound, we'll say so and price a [re-deck](/services/deck-repair) over the structure you already own, which costs meaningfully less than a full [replacement](/services/deck-replacement). Either way you get it in writing.

## The slope work

On the Green Mountain side, grade sets the design. Walk-out lots want elevated decks with engineered posts and footings, and the [railing](/services/railings) decision stops being cosmetic — cable and slim aluminum systems hold a foothills view that pickets would chop into slices. Add the wind that runs down off the hogback and any [pergola or patio cover](/services/pergolas-patio-covers) here has to be detailed for uplift rather than just weight. It's the same engineering we bring to [Castle Pines](/locations/castle-pines) ridges, applied to a different exposure.

The payoff is the reason people buy on that side of town in the first place. A properly built elevated deck on a Green Mountain lot is the best room in the house nine months a year.

## Permits, and which counter you're at

Lakewood runs its own permit process through the city, separate from the county counters that handle most of our south-metro work — and some postal-Lakewood addresses actually sit in unincorporated Jefferson County. We confirm which applies to your address, produce the drawings to that standard, submit, and manage inspections. That's part of every build, not an add-on, and our [permit guide](/deck-permits-south-denver-metro) explains how the process generally runs across the metro.
