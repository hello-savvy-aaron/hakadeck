## Higher, windier, and more exposed

Castle Pines is the highest ground we build on regularly, and the climate math changes with the elevation. At roughly 6,000 feet, on ridges that run out over the Douglas County scrub oak, decks here take more sun, more wind, and bigger temperature swings than the same deck would take twenty minutes north. Boards that merely survive in Denver get punished up here.

That's the practical case for leading with [capped composite](/services/composite-decks) in Castle Pines rather than a matter of taste. The cap shrugs off UV that bleaches stain in a season and hail that pits soft wood, and the [brands we're certified to install](/blog/trex-vs-timbertech-vs-fiberon) warranty it for decades. If you want real wood anyway, we'll build it and tell you honestly what the maintenance clock looks like at this exposure — our [materials comparison](/composite-vs-hardwood-decking-colorado) plays it straight either way.

## Wind is a structural input, not a weather complaint

The single biggest difference between a Castle Pines cover and a flat-lot cover is uplift. A [pergola or patio cover](/services/pergolas-patio-covers) on an exposed ridge behaves like a wing in a spring windstorm, and the loads that matter run upward, not down. We engineer the post connections, footings, and hardware for that reality — which is why our covers here cost more than a bargain bid and why they're still standing after the wind events that take out the bargain ones.

Terrain does the rest of the work on design. Castle Pines lots roll, and walk-out basements are the norm, which puts a lot of our work in [elevated deck](/services/deck-replacement) territory: taller engineered posts, real footings, and [railing](/services/railings) chosen so it holds the view instead of fencing it off. Cable and slim aluminum systems earn their premium on these lots — you didn't buy a ridge-top yard to look at pickets.

## Design review, handled

Castle Pines takes its architectural standards seriously, and homeowners here rarely enjoy the paperwork. We prepare the whole submittal — plan drawings, elevations, material and color specs — file it, and then run the building permit and inspections through the correct counter once design approval comes back. Whether your address permits through the city or unincorporated Douglas County, that's ours to sort out; our [permit guide](/blog/deck-permits-centennial-arapahoe-douglas) covers how the south-metro process works if you want the background.
