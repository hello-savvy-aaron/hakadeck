## Denver's deck builder, from the ground up

Haka Decks builds custom decks across Denver and the whole metro around it. Our shop sits just south of the city in the [Denver Tech Center](/locations/centennial), which puts every Denver neighborhood inside our everyday working radius — Wash Park, Hilltop, Cherry Creek, Congress Park, Berkeley, Sloan's Lake, Park Hill, and the newer builds out in Central Park (Stapleton). We're a specialist deck contractor, not a general remodeler that frames a deck between other jobs, and that focus is exactly why homeowners searching for **custom decks in Denver** end up on the phone with us.

## Different Denver homes, different decks

Denver's housing stock is unusually varied, and the right deck depends heavily on where in the city you are. In the older core — the bungalows and Denver Squares of Wash Park, Congress Park, and Platt Park — the job is usually a tight-lot rebuild that respects the architecture and squeezes real outdoor living out of a modest yard. In Hilltop, Crestmoor, and Cherry Creek, the briefs get larger: multi-zone decks, [outdoor kitchens](/services/outdoor-kitchens), and [covered structures](/services/pergolas-patio-covers) that turn a backyard into a second living room. And in Central Park and the newer northwest neighborhoods, we do a lot of ground-up composite builds on homes whose original builder-grade decks are already failing.

Whatever the neighborhood, the material answer in this climate is usually the same. Denver sits a mile up with 300-plus days of sun, hard hail seasons, and a freeze-thaw cycle that destroys anything built to flatland standards. That's why we lead with [capped composite decking](/services/composite-decks) — it simply outlasts wood here — while still building [cedar and hardwood decks](/services/hardwood-cedar-decks) for homeowners who want natural material and know the maintenance that comes with it.

## Permits, zoning, and HOAs — handled

Deck work inside Denver runs through the City and County of Denver's development services, with zoning setbacks and, in many neighborhoods, historic or design overlays on top. In the suburbs it's a city building division plus an HOA architectural review. Either way, the drawings, permit submission, and inspections are part of every Haka build — not an add-on and not your problem to chase.
