## The detail that makes or breaks a deck

Railing is the most-touched, most-seen part of any deck, and it's the first thing that gives a build away. Done well, it disappears and lets the view through; done poorly, it's the wobble, the rust streaks, and the dated look you can't stop noticing. As a railing contractor working across the Denver and Colorado Springs areas, Haka Decks treats railings as a design decision and a safety system — not a leftover line item at the end of the job.

## Railing styles we install

We build in four main systems, and the right one depends on your view, your budget, and the look you're after. **Cable railing** uses thin stainless lines for a nearly invisible barrier — the choice when you don't want to block a foothills view. **Aluminum railing** is the low-maintenance workhorse: powder-coated, rust-proof, and clean-lined. **Glass-panel railing** gives you a full, unobstructed view and a wind break on an exposed deck. And **composite railing** matches the deck surface for a substantial, integrated look. We'll show you samples and talk through how each one ages in Colorado before you decide.

## Safety and code, handled correctly

Railings are governed by building code for a reason, and inspectors check them closely. Guard height, baluster and cable spacing that a four-inch sphere can't pass through, graspable handrails on stairs, and structural post attachment that won't loosen over time are all non-negotiable. We build every railing to current Colorado code and detail the post connections so they stay rock-solid for the life of the deck. If you've got young kids or you're planning to sell, this is not the place to cut corners — and it's a common failure point we find on older and DIY decks.

## Stairs, grab rails, and accessibility

Stairs are where most railing problems and most accidents happen. We build stair railings and continuous, graspable handrails that meet code and actually feel secure under your hand, plus grab rails and gentler stair geometry where accessibility matters. The transitions between guard rail and stair rail are the fussy details that separate a professional install from a weekend one, and they're exactly the kind of thing we get right.

## Rooftop and roof-deck railings

Roof railing is its own discipline. A rooftop deck or a walkable flat roof needs a guard system that meets code at height, attaches to the structure without compromising the waterproofing membrane, and stands up to full wind and sun exposure with nothing around it for shelter. We build roof-deck railings — cable, aluminum, and glass-panel — with flashed, membrane-safe post mounts and heights detailed for the extra exposure a rooftop sees. Whether it's a true rooftop terrace, a second-story deck, or a balcony rail on an exposed elevation, the roof railing is the part that has to be right the first time, and it's exactly the kind of connection we detail rather than improvise.

## Lighting and the finishing touches

Railing is also where lighting lives. We integrate post-cap lights, recessed stair-riser lights, and under-rail strips during the build, wired and concealed so the deck is safe and beautiful after dark. On a re-rail of an existing deck, we can often upgrade the railing and add lighting without rebuilding the deck surface — a high-impact, lower-cost refresh.
